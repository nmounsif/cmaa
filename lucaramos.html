VIP Guest

SGM Ruth Luka Prince-Ramos Jr.

  • Specialty:TBD
  • Teaching:TBD
  • Date:March 23rd, 2024

A pivotal figure in history, Senior Grandmaster Ruth Luka Prince-Ramos stands at the forefront of a transformative era. Remarkably, she attained the distinction of becoming GGM Sid Ascuncion's second female black belt, while also achieving the monumental feat of being Sijo Emperado's 1st inaugural black belt recipient.
Her legacy is further cemented as an integral member of the legendary Female version of the Horseman of GGM Sid Ascuncion, a formidable group that achieved championship victories on a global scale. With unwavering dedication, she has played a pivotal role in steering the growth and cultivating the core values that define the essence of KenKaBo.
Senior Grandmaster Ruth Luka Prince-Ramos remains a driving force behind the evolution of this discipline, leaving an indelible mark on its legacy.

Early Life and Introduction to Martial Arts Senior Grandmaster Luka Prince-Ramos Jr. was born on June 13, 1947 in Honolulu Hawaii. From a young age, she displayed a natural inclination towards physical activities and a strong determination to pursue excellence. Her journey into the world of martial arts began when she was 15 years old in Honolulu, which soon brought her under the guidance of Great Grand Master Sid Ascuncion. This early exposure ignited a lifelong passion for martial arts and set the foundation for her remarkable journey.
Dedicated Training and Mastery: As a dedicated student, Senior Grandmaster Luka embarked on a rigorous training regimen, devoting countless hours to honing her skills in particular Katas and Point Fighting where she would often be cast against much higher belts and where she would most often WIN.
Her commitment and unwavering discipline caught the attention of Sijo Adriano Emperado, the renowned martial artist recognized for his expertise in Kajukenbo. Under the tutelage of Great Grandmaster Sid Ascuncion, Sijo Emperado and her husband the legendary GGM Sixto Ramos Jr, Senior Grandmaster Luka flourished, mastering Kajukenbo KenKaBo and gaining a deep understanding of the philosophies that underpin her chosen martial art. GGM Sid Ascuncion so valued and admired her talents that he entered her school in 1973 and after 5 YEARS as a Green Belt he personally promoted her to 3rd degree blackbelt and also Head Instructor Rank.
Innovations and Contributions: Senior Grandmaster Luka is widely celebrated for their groundbreaking contributions to the world of martial arts. She is credited with introducing innovative training methodologies, refining traditional techniques, and developing new forms that have elevated the practice to new heights. unique approach to Kajukenbo KenKaBo has inspired generations of practitioners and has left an indelible mark on the martial arts community. Her impeccable style in katas and point fighting have been copied the world over.
Mentorship and Legacy: Beyond her personal achievements, Senior Grandmaster Luka is revered for their role as a mentor and educator, not only in martial arts but in the art of Hula and Tahitian Dance where she is also the equivalent of a Grandmaster. Throughout her illustrious career, she has trained numerous students who have gone on to become esteemed martial artists in their own right. Grandmaster Luka's dedication to passing on their knowledge and values has helped preserve and advance the legacy of martial arts and hula for future generations.
Accolades and Achievements: Senior Grandmaster Luka’s contributions have garnered widespread recognition and accolades. She is recognized as Kajukenbo’s FIRST WOMAN GRANDMASTER. Once recognized as the top woman competitor in Hawaii she earned dozens of local, National and International Titles in her illustrious career. These accolades earned her election into 2 International Martial Arts Hall of Fame. Of note is her election into the Las Vegas Hall of Fame in 2023 for her contributions to martial arts. These accolades solidified her position as a true legend in the world of martial arts.
Philosophy and Vision: At the core of Senior Grandmaster Lukas's martial arts journey lies a profound philosophy that emphasizes the Kajukenbo creed, developing the Mind, Body, and Spirit. She envisions a world where martial arts serve as a means of personal growth, empowerment, and fostering harmony among individuals and communities.
Personal Life: Outside the dojo, Senior Grandmaster Luka is also a World Champion Horse Rider and Kajukenbo and Hawaiian Historian. She is a Kumu Hula which is the equivalent to Grandmaster in Hula and Tahitian Dance. She is an active advocate for Hawaiian Homelands Rights and for Hawaii’s children. Her well-rounded lifestyle reflects the holistic approach she brings to her martial arts practice. She is married to KenKaBo GGM Sixto Ramos Jr. and lives in Honolulu Hawaii.
Conclusion: Senior Grandmaster Ruth Luka Prince-Ramos JR. stands as a paragon of martial arts excellence, a visionary leader who has shaped the landscape of martial arts through innovation, mentorship, and unwavering dedication. Her legacy continues to inspire and guide practitioners around the world, ensuring that Kajukenbo KenKaBo, one of the 4 main and original branches of Kajukenbo, the art she has devoted her life to will endure for generations to come.
Senior Grandmaster Luka remains the embodiment of the profound connection between mind, body, and spirit, demonstrating that the true essence of martial arts extends far beyond physical technique, enriching lives, and communities in profound ways.
